## Our Commitment

llamdrop exists for people who get left out — by cost, by hardware, by complexity. This community reflects that. Everyone here is welcome, regardless of experience level, background, or what device they're running.

## The Standard

Be kind. Be patient. Be useful.

This means:
- Help beginners without making them feel stupid for asking
- Give feedback that is honest and constructive, not dismissive
- Respect that people are working with constrained hardware and limited time
- No gatekeeping. No elitism. No "just buy better hardware."

This means don't:
- Insult, demean, or talk down to anyone
- Harass contributors, maintainers, or users in any space
- Dismiss someone's problem because it seems obvious to you
- Make this space unwelcoming for anyone based on who they are

## Scope

This applies to GitHub Issues, Pull Requests, Discussions, and any space where llamdrop is represented.

## Enforcement

Violations can be reported by opening a private issue or contacting the maintainer directly via GitHub. Reports will be reviewed and handled. Repeated or serious violations result in removal from the project.

## The Short Version

If someone asked for help running AI on their phone because it was the only computer they had — would you help them or make them feel bad for asking? That's the standard here.
